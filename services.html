Services

Makerspaces

I’ve always believed that the best way to learn anything is to do it and coding is no exception. Students in my game design makerspaces will spend most of their time working on projects and very little time listening to me talk. My job is not to force students to learn or babysit their progress. It is to give them the resources and support they need to succeed at their own pace and on their own terms.

If you’re interested in imagining, coding and creating games, I run several game development makerspaces through the Auburn Parks and Recreation department. Information and signup can be found here.

I built a site for students in my makerspaces to provide them resources and track their progress. If you would like to see the coding resources I use (or if you are in one of my classes and forgot the link) it can be found at mymakersite.com. Feel free to use “guestuser” and “1234” to sign in and take a look at what we’ve got so far!

Advanced Coding Classes

In the past I’ve run classes and makerspaces for more advanced topics in web development and scripting languages for middle and high schoolers. Typically these don’t draw enough students to warrant full classes so for now if you’re interested in advanced development, check out the tutoring section.

Tutoring

I offer tutoring services for self-motivated students of any age interested in really getting into coding and development. My goal for these sessions is to make students very good at writing code while giving them as much freedom as possible in choosing the types of languages and projects that most interest them. I have very high expectations of students I work with one-on-one and will ask for some time commitment outside of our sessions in the form of working on assigned projects and typing practice (if they can’t touch type yet).

I’ve tutored students from ages 8 to 58 and anywhere from “absolute beginner” to “college ITS student” in coding ability. I don’t care in the slightest where you’re starting from, only that you care and are willing to put in the work.

I typically charge $30 an hour for tutoring. If that’s a little too expensive and/or if you would like tutoring for more than one student during a session, I charge and additional $5 per hour per person (for instance 3 students would be $40 an hour total but $13 for each).

I really enjoy working with students one on one as it gives us a chance to get into the kind of high level programming that will have substantial application to their lives and future work. Get in touch if that’s something you’re interested in!
