Classes and Makerspaces

Most of my work right now revolves around running Makerspaces for programming and game development. Right now I have a game development class using Scratch for 3-5th grade and one using GameMaker Stuido 2 intended mainly for middle schoolers. These programs are run through Auburn Parks and Recreation and if you're interested in attending, information and signup for sessions can be found here. I sometimes also offer higher level programming classes such as web development if there's enough students interested to warrent a full class.

Tutoring

I offer tutoring services for self-motivated students interested in really getting into coding and development. My goal for these sessions is to make students very good at writing code while giving them as much freedom as possible in choosing the types of languages and projects that will most interest them. I have much higher expectations of students I work with one-on-one and will ask for at least some time commitment outside of our sessions in the form of working at home on assigned projects and/or touch typing practice (something I consider a prerequisite to doing real coding projects).

I have tutored students from ages 8 to 58 and anywhere from absolute beginner to college IT student in coding ability. I really enjoy working with students one on one as it gives us a chance to get into the kinds of high level programming that can have substantial application to their lives and future work. So definitely get in touch if that’s something you’re interested in!
